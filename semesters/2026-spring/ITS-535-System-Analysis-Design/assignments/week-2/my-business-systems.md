## Data Driven System

As a Data Engineer working in mortgage loan servicing, the distinction between data-driven systems and traditional business systems resonates deeply with my daily work. Szukits and Móricz (2024) examine how organizations transform their decision-making routines through data-driven approaches, noting that "the surge in data-related investments has drawn the attention of both managers and academia to the question of whether and how this (re)shapes decision making routines" (p. 2849). This observation captures precisely what differentiates data-driven systems from conventional business systems.

Traditional business systems typically follow predetermined rules and workflows, processing transactions based on established procedures. A conventional loan processing system, for example, routes applications through fixed decision trees based on static criteria. In contrast, data-driven systems fundamentally reshape how decisions emerge within these workflows. Rather than relying solely on predefined logic, data-driven systems continuously analyze patterns, extract insights, and adapt recommendations based on accumulated information.

What struck me most in the research was the finding that analytical culture—the organizational commitment to using data throughout decision-making phases—requires more than just technology investment. Szukits and Móricz (2024) found that "top management support for business analytics and perceived data quality are good predictors of an analytical culture" (p. 2849). This highlights that data-driven systems represent not merely a technical architecture but an organizational transformation. The system must be designed to collect quality data, but equally important, the organization must cultivate practices that embed data analysis into every decision phase.

In my experience building loan analytics pipelines, the most successful implementations occur when the technical system design aligns with organizational readiness to act on data insights rather than override them with intuition.
