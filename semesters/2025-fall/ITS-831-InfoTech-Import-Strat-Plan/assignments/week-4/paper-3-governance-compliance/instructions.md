## IT Governance Framework Overview

### COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies)

**Purpose:** Framework for developing, implementing, monitoring, and improving IT governance and management

**Key Components:**
- 5 Governance Objectives (Evaluate, Direct, Monitor)
- 4 Management Objectives (Align, Plan, Organize; Build, Acquire, Implement; Deliver, Service, Support; Monitor, Evaluate, Assess)
- 40 Governance and Management Processes
- Focus: Align IT with business goals, ensure compliance, optimize resources

**Use Cases:**
- Enterprise-wide IT governance
- Board-level oversight and reporting
- IT value delivery and risk optimization
- Regulatory compliance (SOX, GDPR, HIPAA)

**Example:** Financial institution using COBIT to ensure IT investments support business strategy while maintaining SOX compliance

---

### ISO 27001 (Information Security Management System)

**Purpose:** International standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an information security management system (ISMS)

**Key Components:**
- Risk assessment and treatment methodology
- Statement of Applicability (SoA) - selecting relevant controls
- 93 security controls across 14 domains (Annex A)
- PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act) for continuous improvement

**Use Cases:**
- Information security governance
- Third-party trust (certification demonstrates security posture)
- GDPR Article 32 compliance (appropriate technical and organizational measures)
- Multi-site global security consistency

**Example:** Cloud provider obtaining ISO 27001 certification to demonstrate security controls to enterprise customers

---

### NIST Frameworks

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF):**
- **Purpose:** Voluntary framework for managing cybersecurity risk
- **Core Functions:** Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
- **Tiers:** Implementation maturity levels (Partial → Adaptive)
- **Profiles:** Current and target state cybersecurity posture
- **Use Cases:** Critical infrastructure, federal agencies, private sector adoption

**NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) - NIST SP 800-37:**
- **Purpose:** Mandatory framework for U.S. federal systems
- **Steps:** Prepare, Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor
- **Integration:** NIST SP 800-53 security controls (including SR family from Week 4 discussion)
- **Use Cases:** Federal agencies, contractors, FedRAMP cloud providers

**NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF):**
- **Purpose:** Framework for managing risks of AI systems
- **Functions:** Govern, Map, Measure, Manage
- **Use Cases:** Organizations deploying AI/ML systems
- **Note:** Discussed in Week 2 AI risk management exercise

**Example:** Healthcare organization using NIST CSF to structure cybersecurity program while complying with HIPAA Security Rule

---

### Other Relevant Frameworks

**ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library):**
- Service management framework
- Focus on IT service delivery and operations
- Complements governance frameworks like COBIT

**SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2):**
- Attestation for service providers (especially SaaS, cloud)
- Trust Services Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy
- Critical for B2B trust

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation):**
- EU data protection regulation with global reach
- Not a framework but requires governance for compliance
- Privacy by Design and Default principles

**PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard):**
- Mandatory for organizations handling credit card data
- 12 requirements across 6 control objectives

**FedRAMP (Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program):**
- Standardized approach to security assessment for U.S. government cloud services
- Based on NIST SP 800-53 controls

- ✅ Industry reports from reputable sources (Gartner, Forrester - for context, not as primary sources)
- ❌ Blog posts, magazine articles (not scholarly)

### Connecting Governance to Business Strategy

**Don't just describe frameworks - analyze strategic impact:**

❌ Weak: "COBIT has 40 processes that organizations implement."
✅ Strong: "By implementing COBIT's APO02 (Manage Strategy) process, [Organization] aligned IT investment priorities with business objectives, resulting in a 15% reduction in duplicative technology spending while accelerating time-to-market for customer-facing applications."

❌ Weak: "ISO 27001 requires risk assessments."
✅ Strong: "ISO 27001's risk-based approach enabled [Organization] to prioritize security investments in customer-facing APIs and payment systems—the highest-risk, highest-business-value assets—while applying lighter controls to internal tools, optimizing both security and innovation velocity."

### Balancing Compliance and Innovation Themes

**Show tension AND resolution:**

**Tension Example:**
> "Traditional ISO 27001 change management controls—requiring formal review boards and extensive testing—conflicted with [Organization]'s DevOps culture emphasizing rapid deployment cycles. This created friction between security governance requirements and development team velocity."

**Resolution Example:**
> "To reconcile this tension, [Organization] implemented automated security testing integrated into CI/CD pipelines, enabling continuous compliance verification without manual gates. Changes passing automated security scans could deploy immediately, while only high-risk changes (customer data access, payment processing) required formal change advisory board review. This risk-tiered approach satisfied ISO 27001 requirements while preserving development agility."

### Global vs. Regional Regulatory Considerations

**Demonstrate understanding of regulatory complexity:**

- **GDPR (EU):** Extraterritorial reach, strict consent, data subject rights, DPIAs for high-risk processing
- **CCPA/CPRA (California):** Consumer privacy rights, similar to GDPR but less stringent
- **HIPAA (US Healthcare):** Protected health information security and privacy
- **GLBA (US Financial Services):** Financial privacy, safeguards rule
- **PCI-DSS (Global):** Payment card data protection
- **SOX (US Public Companies):** Financial reporting and IT controls
- **China Cybersecurity Law:** Data localization, network operator obligations
- **Russia Data Localization:** Russian citizen data must be stored in Russia

**Show how frameworks help:**
> "ISO 27001's control framework provided [Organization] with a unified information security baseline applicable across all operating regions. While GDPR required specific privacy controls (data minimization, consent management), HIPAA mandated additional safeguards for health data, and China's Cybersecurity Law imposed data localization, the foundational ISO 27001 ISMS enabled consistent risk assessment and control application across jurisdictions, with region-specific enhancements layered on top."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Selecting an organization with insufficient public information**
- Choose companies with published governance reports, case studies, or significant media coverage

❌ **Only describing frameworks without analyzing their application**
- Don't just explain what COBIT is; analyze how the organization uses it strategically

❌ **Ignoring the 6 required questions**
- Explicitly address each question; consider using them as section headers

❌ **Generic analysis not specific to your selected organization**
- Use specific examples, incidents, reports from your chosen company

❌ **Treating governance as purely technical**
- Connect to business strategy, competitive advantage, organizational culture

❌ **Overlooking emerging technology challenges**
- Must address AI and cloud compliance challenges (Question 2)

❌ **Using only course materials without scholarly articles**
- Minimum 2 scholarly journal articles required beyond textbook

❌ **Poor APA formatting**
- Review APA7 guidelines for citations and references

❌ **Weak conclusion that just repeats introduction**
- Synthesize insights, provide recommendations, look forward
