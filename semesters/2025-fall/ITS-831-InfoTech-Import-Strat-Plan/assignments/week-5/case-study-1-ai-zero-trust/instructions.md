## Assignment Overview

The NIST Special Publication 800-207, Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) provides a framework for organizations to modernize their cybersecurity posture by shifting from perimeter-based defenses to a model based on continuous verification, least privilege access, and strict identity management. ZTA is built around core pillars such as Identity, Devices, Network/Environment, Application Workload, Data, and Visibility & Analytics.

Additionally, the Zero Trust Maturity Model (ZTMM) developed by CISA outlines phases of maturity:

- Traditional
- Initial
- Advanced
- Optimal (characterized by dynamic, risk-based, automated controls and protections)

One of the significant enablers to reaching the Optimal maturity level is the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) to automate detection, decision-making, and enforcement in real time.
