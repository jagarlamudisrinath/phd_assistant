This is just to summarize association/correlation conclusions:

## Is there a relationship?

- **p-value > 0.05**: there is no relationship between the 2 variables
- **p-value ≤ 0.05**: there is a relationship between the 2 variables

## Type of relationship:

- **None**: No correlation detected
- **Positive**: Both variables increase together (or decrease together)
- **Negative**: When one increases, the other decreases (or vice versa)

## Visually:

For visual examples of correlation types, see:
[Math is Fun - Correlation](https://www.mathsisfun.com/data/correlation.html)

## Can we estimate the impact of a variable on the other using correlation?

**No.** And that's why we move to inferential statistics using modeling (e.g., linear or logistic regression).
