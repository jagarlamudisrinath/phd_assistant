# Association vs Causation

**From: Dr. Anderson**

---

Hi Everyone,

I wanted to stress the differences between association and causation.

## Association (Correlation)

**Definition:** Two variables move together, but not necessarily because one influences the other.

**Example:** People who buy sports gear are less likely to develop heart failure.

**Key Idea:** There's a link, but it could be:
- Coincidental
- A shared underlying cause (a confounder)
- Reverse causality (the presumed effect is actually the cause)

## Causality (Cause and Effect)

**Definition:** One variable directly brings about a change in another.

**Example:** A bowling ball rolling down the lane causes the pins to fall.

**Key Idea:** For a causal relationship to exist:
- The cause must happen **before** the effect
- There must be a **clear mechanism** for how the cause leads to the effect

## Important Note

**A hypothesis test showing a difference between pre- and post-treatment results indicates an association, but it does not, by itself, prove causation.**
