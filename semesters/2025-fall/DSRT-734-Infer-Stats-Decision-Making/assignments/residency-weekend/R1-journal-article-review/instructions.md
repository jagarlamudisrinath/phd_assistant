## CRITICAL REQUIREMENT: Statistical Reporting

**You must report at least one statistic/result from the paper using correct APA formatting.**

### APA Statistical Reporting Format

When reporting statistical results, use the proper APA format. If you don't know a piece of information (e.g., degrees of freedom or test statistic), use an asterisk `*` for the missing part.

**Example:**
> "The study showed a significant difference between the means..., (F[2, *]=*, p=0.023)."

**Common Statistical Report Formats:**
- **ANOVA:** (F[df₁, df₂] = value, p = value)
- **t-test:** (t[df] = value, p = value)
- **Chi-square:** (χ²[df] = value, p = value)
- **Correlation:** (r[df] = value, p = value)
- **Regression:** (β = value, t[df] = value, p = value) or (R² = value, F[df₁, df₂] = value, p = value)

## Required Paper Components

Your review must include the following sections in APA format:

### 1. Introduction/Background

**Purpose:** Provide context for the research article and explain what motivated the study.

**Address the following:**
- What led the author(s) to write this piece?
- What was the research gap or problem being addressed?
- What key concepts were explored?
- Were there weaknesses in prior research that led the author to the current hypothesis or research question?
- What is the significance of this research area?

**Length:** Typically 0.5 - 1 page

---

### 2. Methodology

**Purpose:** Describe the research design, data collection, and analytical approach.

**Address the following:**
- What research questions or hypotheses were the researchers trying to explore?
- What was the study design (experimental, observational, survey, etc.)?
- Who were the participants/subjects? How many? How were they selected?
- How was the data gathered?
  - What instruments or measures were used?
  - What variables were measured?
- What statistical analysis was used?
  - Descriptive statistics?
  - Inferential tests (t-tests, ANOVA, regression, chi-square, etc.)?
  - Software used (SPSS, R, SAS, etc.)?
- Were there any notable methodological considerations or limitations?

**Length:** Typically 0.75 - 1.5 pages

---

### 3. Study Findings and Results

**Purpose:** Summarize the major findings and outcomes of the research.

**Address the following:**
- What were the major findings from the study?
- Were the hypotheses supported or rejected?
- What were the key statistical results?
  - **Include at least one properly formatted statistical result (see CRITICAL REQUIREMENT above)**
  - Report effect sizes if provided
  - Note significance levels
- Were there any unexpected findings?
- What limitations did the authors acknowledge?
  - Sample size limitations?
  - Generalizability concerns?
  - Measurement limitations?
  - Other constraints?

**Length:** Typically 0.75 - 1.5 pages

---

### 4. Conclusions

**Purpose:** Evaluate the article critically and assess its contribution to the field.

**Address the following:**

**Significance:**
- How significant are the findings to the field?
- Do the results advance knowledge or practice?
- Are there practical implications?

**Research Methods:**
- Were the research methods appropriate for the questions asked?
- Were there strengths in the methodological approach?
- Were there weaknesses or alternative methods you would have chosen?
- Was the sample size adequate for the statistical tests used?

**Statistical Analysis:**
- Was the statistical analysis appropriate and rigorous?
- Were assumptions of statistical tests met or addressed?
- Were effect sizes and practical significance discussed?
- Were there limitations in the statistical approach?

**Readability and Presentation:**
- Was the article well-written and clearly organized?
- Were the results presented clearly (tables, figures, text)?
- Could a practitioner understand and apply the findings?

**Implications and Future Research:**
- Does the piece lead into further study?
- What questions remain unanswered?
- What are the broader implications of the results?

**Overall Evaluation:**
- What are the overall strengths and weaknesses of the article?
- Would you recommend this article to others in your field?

**Length:** Typically 0.75 - 1.5 pages

---

## Writing Tips

### General Guidelines

1. **Use third person** - Avoid "I" or "we" (e.g., "The researcher found..." not "I found...")
2. **Be objective** - Critically evaluate but maintain professional tone
3. **Be concise** - 2-5 pages means focus on key points
4. **Use clear transitions** - Help the reader follow your analysis
5. **Proofread carefully** - Grammar and spelling matter at the doctoral level

### APA Formatting Reminders

- **No cover page** for this assignment
- **Headings:** Use APA Level 1 headings for main sections
- **In-text citations:** (Author, Year) or Author (Year)
- **References:** Hanging indent, alphabetical order
- **Font:** 12-pt Times New Roman or similar
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Margins:** 1-inch on all sides

### Statistical Reporting Tips

- **Always include:**
  - Test statistic (t, F, χ², r, etc.)
  - Degrees of freedom (if applicable)
  - p-value
  - Direction of effect (which group was higher, positive/negative correlation)

- **If information is missing:**
  - Use asterisks: (F[2, *]=*, p=0.023)
  - Explain in text: "The authors reported a significant effect (p < 0.05) but did not provide full statistical details."

- **Italicize statistical symbols:** Use *F*, *t*, *p*, *r*, *M*, *SD*, etc.

## Example Statistical Reporting

Here are examples of how to report different types of statistics in your review:

### t-test Example
> "The independent samples t-test revealed a significant difference in job satisfaction between remote workers (*M* = 4.2, *SD* = 0.8) and office workers (*M* = 3.6, *SD* = 0.9), *t*(148) = 4.23, *p* < 0.001."

### ANOVA Example
> "A one-way ANOVA indicated significant differences in performance across the three training conditions, *F*(2, 87) = 5.67, *p* = 0.005, η² = 0.12."

### Correlation Example
> "The Pearson correlation analysis showed a strong positive relationship between employee engagement and productivity, *r*(245) = 0.68, *p* < 0.001."

### Regression Example
> "Multiple regression analysis revealed that training hours significantly predicted job performance, β = 0.45, *t*(198) = 6.23, *p* < 0.001, with the model explaining 34% of the variance, *R²* = 0.34, *F*(3, 196) = 33.78, *p* < 0.001."

### When Information is Missing
> "The chi-square test indicated a significant association between education level and promotion rates, χ²[*] = *, *p* = 0.012, suggesting that employees with advanced degrees were more likely to receive promotions."
