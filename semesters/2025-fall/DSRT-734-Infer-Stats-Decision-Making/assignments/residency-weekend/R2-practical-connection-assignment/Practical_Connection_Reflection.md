## Practical Connection Reflection

As a data engineer and analyst working in the capital marketing team at Lakeview Loan Servicing, a mortgage servicing company, I am consistently tasked with analyzing complex datasets to support strategic business decisions. The knowledge and skills acquired through DSRT-734 Inferential Statistics in Decision-Making have proven invaluable in enhancing my ability to apply rigorous statistical methods to real-world challenges. This reflection explores how the course's core concepts—probability theory, hypothesis testing, correlation and regression analysis, and research design—have been directly applied to my work, particularly in developing data-driven loan offer generation strategies that respond to changing capital market conditions.

One of my primary projects involves optimizing loan offer generation for mortgage customers by strategically reducing interest rates when market conditions in the capital markets shift favorably. This work requires a deep understanding of probability theory and statistical estimation, concepts thoroughly covered in this course (Fuster et al., 2019). Before taking DSRT-734, my approach to analyzing market trends was largely descriptive, relying on basic summary statistics and historical averages. However, the course's emphasis on probability distributions and confidence intervals has transformed how I assess market volatility and predict future rate movements. I now apply probability theory to model the likelihood of various market scenarios, enabling our team to make more informed decisions about the timing and magnitude of rate adjustments. By calculating confidence intervals for projected market rates, I can provide the capital marketing team with statistically sound recommendations that quantify the uncertainty inherent in our forecasts, ultimately leading to more prudent risk management.

Hypothesis testing, another cornerstone of the course, has become an essential tool in validating the effectiveness of our rate reduction strategies. When we propose adjusting interest rates to attract more customers, I design experiments to test specific hypotheses about customer response rates (Lane, 2023). For instance, I recently conducted a parametric analysis using independent samples t-tests to determine whether customers offered reduced rates showed significantly higher acceptance rates compared to a control group receiving standard offers. The course taught me how to properly formulate null and alternative hypotheses, select appropriate significance levels, and interpret p-values in the context of business decisions. This rigorous approach has added credibility to our recommendations and helped the organization avoid costly decisions based on anecdotal evidence or intuition alone. Understanding Type I and Type II errors has also made me more cautious and ethical in my interpretations, ensuring that I do not overstate findings or mislead stakeholders with false positives.

Regression and correlation analysis have been particularly transformative in my daily work. In the loan offer generation project, I developed multiple regression models to examine the relationship between various predictor variables—such as current market interest rates, customer credit scores, loan-to-value ratios, and regional economic indicators—and the dependent variable of customer acceptance rates (Sirignano et al., 2021). The course's coverage of simple and multiple regression, including assumptions testing and interpretation of coefficients, has enabled me to build robust predictive models that inform our pricing strategies. I now routinely assess multicollinearity, check residual plots for homoscedasticity, and validate model fit using R-squared and adjusted R-squared values. These techniques, learned through course exercises and assignments, have empowered me to move beyond correlation to understanding causation, thereby supporting the capital marketing team in making evidence-based decisions that optimize both customer acquisition and profitability.

Finally, the course's focus on research design and the application of statistical methods to organizational problems has fundamentally improved how I structure my analyses. I have learned to approach each business question as a research problem, carefully defining the population of interest, selecting appropriate sampling methods, and determining whether parametric or nonparametric tests are suitable given the data characteristics. This structured methodology ensures that my analyses are reproducible, transparent, and aligned with best practices in quantitative research. Moreover, the emphasis on critically evaluating statistical information in professional literature has enhanced my ability to assess third-party research reports and industry studies, allowing me to integrate external evidence with internal data to support comprehensive decision-making.

In conclusion, the knowledge and skills gained from DSRT-734 have not only enhanced my technical proficiency but have also shaped my approach to ethical and servant-leadership in data analytics. By applying inferential statistics rigorously and transparently, I contribute to decisions that ultimately benefit mortgage customers through fair, data-driven pricing strategies. The course has equipped me to link research with practice and knowledge with ethical decision-making, fulfilling the university's mission to develop professionals who serve their communities with integrity and competence.
