## Key Concepts to Know

### When to Use Each Test

| Test | Use When |
|------|----------|
| **Pearson's correlation** | Two continuous variables, testing relationship |
| **Spearman's rho** | One or both variables are ordinal, or data not normally distributed |
| **Pearson's Chi-Square** | Categorical variables, 2x2 or larger contingency table, adequate expected cell counts |
| **Fisher's Exact Test** | Categorical variables, 2x2 table with small expected cell counts (<5) |
| **Independent samples t-test** | Comparing means of two independent groups, continuous DV, normal distribution |
| **Paired samples t-test** | Comparing means of same subjects measured twice, continuous DV, normal distribution |
| **ANOVA** | Comparing means of 3+ groups, continuous DV, normal distribution |
| **Mann-Whitney U** | Non-parametric alternative to independent t-test |
| **Wilcoxon signed-rank** | Non-parametric alternative to paired t-test |
