# Scientific Article Critique - Focus on Statistics

**Course:** DSRT 734 - Inferential Statistics in Decision-making

---

## General Article Critique

### Identify the study purpose

- Is the research question clear and relevant?
- Does the article state explicit hypotheses or objectives?

### Assess the study design

- Is the design appropriate for answering the research question (e.g., RCT, cohort, case–control, cross-sectional)?
- Are strengths and limitations of the design discussed?

### Evaluate the sample

- Was the sample size adequate (any power/sample size justification)?
- Was the sampling method appropriate and unbiased?
- Are inclusion and exclusion criteria clear?

---

## Critiquing the Statistical Methods Section

### Clarity & Transparency

- Are the statistical methods described clearly and in enough detail for replication?
- Is the choice of statistical tests justified based on data type and study design?

### Appropriateness of Methods

- Were the statistical tests chosen appropriate for the research question(s) and level of measurement (e.g., chi-square for categorical, t-test/ANOVA for continuous)?
- Were assumptions of each test checked (normality, homogeneity of variance, independence)?
- Were advanced methods (e.g., regression, SEM, survival analysis) used correctly?

### Handling of Variables

- Were dependent and independent variables clearly defined?
- Were confounders or covariates identified and controlled (e.g., via regression, stratification, matching)?
- Were composite measures or scales validated?

### Reporting & Interpretation

- Are descriptive statistics (means, SD, medians, proportions) presented clearly?
- Are effect sizes, confidence intervals, and p-values reported (not just "significant" vs. "not significant")?
- Are multiple testing corrections mentioned if many comparisons were made?
- Are results interpreted appropriately without overstating significance?

### Reproducibility

- Are statistical software and version reported?
- Are analytic decisions (e.g., missing data handling, outlier management) transparent?

### Results & Discussion Alignment

- Do the statistical results align with the stated hypotheses and research questions?
- Does the discussion accurately reflect the strength of evidence (without overgeneralization)?
- Are limitations in the statistical approach acknowledged?

---

## Final Judgment

### Strengths
Note clear justifications, robust analytic strategies, transparency.

### Weaknesses
Point out inappropriate tests, lack of assumption checks, missing effect sizes/CI, or poor reporting.

### Suggestions
Recommend alternative analyses, better reporting, or clearer alignment between methods and conclusions.
